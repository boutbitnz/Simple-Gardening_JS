CABBAGE

It's a very good idea to consider Raised beds when planting Vegetable. The spacing really matters, such that when the vegetables are fully grown, their leaves just barely touch each other, creating a micro climate in which weed growth is suppressed and moisture is conserved. Raised beds produce a variety of benefits: they extend the planting season, they can reduce weeds if designed and planted properly, and they reduce the need to use poor native soil. Since the gardener does not walk on the raised beds, the soil is not compacted and the roots have an easier time growing. Raised Beds; Are created by building wide, flat top rows about 2 ft wide and in high. The soil is raised above the surrounding soil.
